Sir Seretse Khama

The man who led Botswana's independence movement and transition from British rule into an independent nation.

Sir Seretse Khama(front) and his wife Ruth Williams Khama(behind) being joyfully welcomed by the people of Botswana.

Here's a time line of Sir Seretse Khama's life:

  • 1921 - Born in Serowe, Bechuanaland [now Botswana].

  • 1925 - At the age of 4, Seretse became kgosi (king), with his uncle Tshekedi Khama as his regent and guardian.

  • 1944 - Graduated with a general B.A., after being educated in his youth at the Tiger Kloof Educational Institute in South Africa, Khama attended Fort Hare University College there.

  • 1946 - He joined the Inner Temple in London, to study to become a barrister.

  • 1947 - Khama met Ruth Williams, an English clerk at Lloyd's of London. After a year of courtship, they married. The interracial marriage sparked an uproar, alarming both the Union of South Africa, which had established legal apartheid (racial segregation), and the tribal elders of the Bamangwato, who were angered he had not chosen one of their women.

  • 1949 - After a series of kgotlas (public meetings), he was reaffirmed by the elders in his role as the kgosi (king), due to his marriage to Ruth.

  • 1951 - Khama and his wife weere exiled from Bechuanaland by the British government.

  • 1956 - Both Khama and his wife were allowed to return to Bechuanaland as private citizens, after he had renounced the tribal throne. He became involved in local politics, being elected to the tribal council as its secretary, one year later.

  • 1961 - Khama founded the Bechuanaland Democratic Party(BDP). His exile gave him an increased credibility with an independence-minded electorate, and the BDP swept aside its Socialist and Pan-Africanist rivals to dominate the elections 5 years later, and he became the Prime Minister.

  • 1965 - As Prime Minister of Bechuanaland, Khama continued to push for Botswana's independence. A 1965 constitution delineated a new Botswana government, and on 30 September 1966, Botswana gained its independence. Ten days prior to this, Elizabeth II had promoted Khama within the Order of the British Empire, appointing him a Knight Commander (KBE).

  • 1966 - At the time of its independence, Botswana was the world's third-poorest country, poorer than most other African countries. Khama set out on a vigorous economic programme intended to transform the nation into an export-based economy, built around beef, copper and diamonds.

  • 1980 - Khama died in his sleep in the presence of his wife in Botswana.
